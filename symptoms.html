Common Anorexia Symptoms

Physical Symptoms:

  • Extreme weight loss.
  • Fatigue and lack of energy.
  • Dizziness or faiting.
  • Brittle hair and nails or hair thinning.
  • Lanugo (fine, soft hair growth on the body as the body tries to stay warm).
  • Cold intolerance, especially in extremities (hands and feet).
  • Amenorrhea (absence of menstruation in women and girls).
  • Low blood pressure, slow heart rate, or irregular heart rythyms.
  • Gastrointestinal problems, such as constipation or bloating.
  • Muscle weakness and loss of bone density.

Behavioral Symptoms:

  • Restricting food intake, even when hungry.
  • Intense fear of gaining weight or being "fat".
  • Preoccupation with food, calories, or dieting.
  • Excessive exercising or compulsive physical activity.
  • Avoidance of meals or eating in front of others.
  • Ritualistic eating behaviors, such as cutting food into tiny pieces or eating very slowly.
  • Denial of hunger or making excuses to avoid eating.

Emotional and Psychological Symptoms:

  • Distorted body image (seeing oneself as overweight when underweight).
  • Low self-esteem tied to weight or body shape.
  • Mood changes: mood swings, irritability, or depression.
  • Isolation from family and friends.
  • Perfectionism or excessive need for control.
